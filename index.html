Tribute to a legend

  • IK (imran khan) born in 5 October 1952
  • IK was born to a Pashtun family in Lahore in 1952
  • Graduated from Keble College, Oxford in 1975
  • He began his international cricket career at age 18, in a 1971 Test series against England.
  • Captain of the Pakistan national cricket team b/w 1982 and 1992
  • IK registered 3,807 runs and took 362 wickets in Test cricket and was inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame.
  • He led to victory in the 1992 Cricket World Cup
  • He was chancellor of the University of Bradford in the United Kingdom from 2005 to 2014
  • In 1991, he launched a fundraising campaign to set up a cancer hospital in memory of his mother
  • IK founded Namal College in 2008.Imran Khan also served as the chancellor of the University of Bradford between 2005 and 2014, and was the recipient of an honorary fellowship by the Royal College of Physicians in 2012.
  • IK founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) in 1996 In regional politics, PTI led a coalition government in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa from 2013, with Khan delegating this leadership to Mahmood Khan after being elected as Prime Minister in 2018
  • As Prime Minister, Khan addressed a balance of payments crisis with a bailout from the International Monetary Fund
